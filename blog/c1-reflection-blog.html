Madeleine's blog post on the cultural values of the DBC/EDA experience

25 May 2016

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience? I think it's a very interesting approach to tackling the problem of how best to fairly quickly tranform a mixed bag of 'normal' people into the people with both technical and soft skills needed by industry. It's almost the opposite of a traditional apprenticeship model for vocational education such as trades training.

<

I did like the 'consumer in a restaurant' versus 'family member contributing to a communal meal' analogy. The inherent contradiction of applying a free market model to education has long troubled me - I think it drastically increases the passivity of traditional learning environments, especially undergraduate university study. It turns it into a transaction with money on one side and education delivered in easily digested, bite-sized chunks on the other.

<

One caveat to the analogy... I think it can be easy to forget that there are a lot of unwritten, non financial contracts at play in a family - there are a lot of expectations of everybody in that kitchen, whether they're scrubbing pototoes or coordinating the whole meal.

What are your impressions? One of my most immediate impressions is the depth of belief in the 'mission' held by everybody involved. I can't help but be impressed by the commitment and enthusiam, even though I'm naturally a bit of a cynic.

<

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture? Well, I've spent a <lot of time studying in a university context and I'm definitely ready to try something different. That said, my training as a scientist is to question everything - that would be the cynic bit - so it will be interesting to see how I engage with some aspects of the learning model.

<

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how? I went along to an open day earlier this year, so I think I had a pretty good idea of what I was getting into. The question really is how I will change to make the most of EDA.

<

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous? To some extent I've already answered these two questions - I'm really interested in seeing what I can get out of (and contribute to) this kind of particpant-driven learning set-up, but slightly concerned that my cynicism will get in the way of full engagement with some aspects.
